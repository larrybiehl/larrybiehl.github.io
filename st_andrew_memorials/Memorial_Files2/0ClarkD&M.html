Delmar Byers Clark

April 27, 1927 to January 11, 2009

Mary Jane (Gardner) Clark

August 27, 1929 to November 21,2012

 

Relationship to St. Andrew United Methodist Church:

            They were the parents of Carol Clark-Elliott a member of St. Andrew.

 

Family:

            Delmar and Mary Jane were parents to seven children.  In order of birth, they are:

Carol, Rich, Bob, Kay, Mary, Jay and Tom.  They had 22 grandchildren and 7 great grandchildren.

            The Clarks lived all their lives on farms in Lawrence County, Pennsylvania.  Their milk, Clark’s Milk, was sold at schools and in grocery stores and was delivered door-to-door.  The Clarks were members of Bethel United Presbyterian Church in Enon Valley, Pennsylvania.

 

Special Recognition:

            During the funeral for his Dad, Rich Clark presented the following poem he had written:

            My dad was a simple man.

            My dad was a wise man.

 

            My dad is gone.

 

 

            My dad was a common man.

            My dad was a rare man.

           

            My dad is still here.

 

            He lives on in his seven grown children,

                                    His grandchildren, the

                                    Family and friends who knew him,

                                    And all the lives he touched.

 

Memorial:  Family was the most important part of Delmar and Mary Jane's lives.  Nothing pleased them more than coming together where they could enjoy a meal and fellowship with family and friends.  Outdoor living was a regular part of their lives..  For these reasons, Carol, her Mother, and her brothers and sisters established a fund to build a picnic shelter at St. Andrew.  While other funds and memorial gifts have also been added to the fund, the primary donors for the shelter were the Delmar and Mary Jane Clark Family.

 

 

 

The Picnic Shelter completed July 2013
